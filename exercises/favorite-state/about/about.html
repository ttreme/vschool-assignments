Description of Guadalajara

Guadalajara (/ˌɡwɑːdələˈhɑːrə/,[3] Spanish pronunciation: [ɡwaðalaˈxaɾa] ( listen)) is the capital and largest city of the Mexican state of Jalisco, and the seat of the municipality of Guadalajara. The city is in the central region of Jalisco in the Western-Pacific area of Mexico. With a population of 1,495,189 it is Mexico's fourth most populous municipality.[4] The Guadalajara Metropolitan Area includes seven adjacent municipalities with a reported population of 4,328,584 in 2009, making it the second most populous metropolitan area in Mexico, behind Mexico City.[5][6] The municipality is the second most densely populated in Mexico, the first being Ciudad Nezahualcóyotl in the State of Mexico.[7] It is a strong business and economic center in the Bajio region.[8][9]
